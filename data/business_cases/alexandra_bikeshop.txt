Alexandra's Bikeshop is a small Swiss SME selling bicycles and offering repair services.
The company has two physical stores, a basic Shopify website, and a small Instagram following.
Revenue has plateaued in the last two years. Younger customers expect online configurators,
home delivery, and trade-in options that the shop currently does not provide. Repair scheduling
is still done by phone, leading to long lead times and unhappy customers.

The owner wants to:
  - improve customer experience across online and in-store touchpoints,
  - increase sales by reaching new customer segments (e-bike commuters, families),
  - reduce operational costs in the repair workflow,
  - build a small but real digital marketing presence,
  - prepare the business for a future second-generation owner who is more digital-native.

Constraints:
  - limited IT budget (under CHF 60k for year 1),
  - 8 employees, none with deep digital skills,
  - no in-house developers,
  - data scattered across POS, Excel files, and paper repair tickets.
